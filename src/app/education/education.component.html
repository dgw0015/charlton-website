From the moment I switched majors to Hospitality, I knew I was in the right place. Through the dedication and passion I witnessed from the professors and the opportunities they fought so hard for us to have access to, I knew that I was going to get the best education and the best experiences from them. I watched my brother go through the program before me, I too witnessed his passion grow more and more for hospitality and I wanted to be a part of it as well. Through the advisory boards, guest lecturers, study abroad opportunities, wine education, culinary education, and much more that the program provides, I can feel confident in entering the hospitality industry knowing that I was provided with the tools to succeed.

Helpful courses from the program

Event Operations

I was able to learn the various components that are important to the planning and operations of hospitality events. Topics such as strategic planning, event design, logistics, staging, audience accommodation, and food and beverage were discussed.

Food Production

Food Production in Hospitality provided me with theoretical and management concepts surrounding front and back of the house culinary operations. The other students and I were given the ability to demonstrate standards, techniques, and practices by creating a meal service event, including the menu development, ticket sales, food production, cost analysis, and service of meals.

Special Events

I learned about the intricacies of special events and I engaged in the planning, management, execution, and evaluation of an event. The course “client” that the course had was the Samuel Ginn College of Engineering, Office of Career Development and Corporate Relations.

Food and Beverage Management

I developed a better understanding of the food service industry and the management of food and beverage operations within the industry. I learned about the topics regarding current issues and trends affecting the industry, food safety, safe environments for workers and consumers, menu planning, conceptual design and site selection, purchasing, receiving, storing, and inventory, food and beverage production, and service.

Catering and Events Management

This course emphasizes the management and organizational techniques employed in the catering and special events industry. I was given a background to the events management industry and I learned about the art of catering and special events.
