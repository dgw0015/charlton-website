Floral Design

One of my favorite seasons is Spring, not for the activities and holidays that happen but because of the flowers that bloom during those times. Seeing the creativity, personality, and ingenuity shine through each and every one of C. Wayman Design and Production’s arrangements sparked my love for floral design. When I first started working for them, I learned the basics of creating arrangements by helping them cut flowers, learning the names of the flowers, and understanding color schemes and matching arrangements to the entire event. Now that I’ve been working there for over a year, they’ve allowed me to help design smaller arrangements and use my own creativity and designs to create beautiful arrangements. In my free time, I put together arrangements for my apartment every chance I get from the leftover flowers we had at work. While it forces me to think outside the box because the variety of flowers changes every week, it serves as pretty décor for my counter tops and acts as a great stress reliever too!

Culinary

The smell of curry, five space, and ginger was constantly floating around in our house. Growing up in a Trinidadian household, food was always cooking on the stove from the moment I woke up. My grandfather owned a seafood restaurant in Trinidad and fortunately for us he lived with us when we moved to Auburn. I always wanted to know what he was cooking so every chance I got I was at the sink washing vegetables and cutting onions to help in any way that I could. Cooking is a form of art and requires a lot of skill, instinct, and imagination. The first thing I turned to on the TV was The Food Network Channel and I was constantly watching Chopped, Cake Boss, Iron Chef, Cutthroat Kitchen, and Worst Cooks in America. These shows taught me so much about cooking techniques, ingredients, names of dishes, and most importantly that there is no limit to what you can do with food. Ever since then I’ve been experimenting and cooking as much as I can and hopefully, I can go to culinary school someday.

Wine

Wine From my internship at Maysara Winery, I developed a new passion and respect for wine. Watching and learning and being hands on with the biodynamic winery, it opened my eyes to the ample amount of opportunities of how wine is produced, where it can be produced, and the complexity of the process. Because of this, I plan on furthering my studies through the Court of Master Sommeliers Course and hopefully continuing on to obtain my Certified Specialist of Wine certification. Not only do I love a challenge, but what better way to be educated about something so simple yet so extremely complex than to sample wines from all over the world.
